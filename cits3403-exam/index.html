Overview - Songversation

Songversation is a game that allows users to log in with their Spotify account, choose a playlist or followed artist, and guess the correct song from three presented lyrics. Points are earned for each correct guess, and the game continues until an incorrect guess is made.

Our website is designed for music lovers who enjoy exploring various genres, artists, and songs. These individuals likely have an active Spotify account and actively curate playlists or follow their favorite artists.

This game offers an entertaining way for users to test their knowledge of songs in their Spotify library. It adds a competitive element as players aim for a high score by correctly guessing as many songs as possible. Unlike Heardle or Lyricle, it provides a personalized experience based on the user's own music collection.

Songversation is built using Flask, using the Spotify API. By leveraging the user's logged-in Spotify data, Songversation allows users to choose from playlists and artists that they follow. Songversation also makes use of the Spotify API to get all the songs belonging to an artist or playlist and then get their lyrics to send to the client to display on their page in the game, as well as cache the lyrics in our own database to speed up loading time for users.

Suggested Features

Vs. Multiplayer

Overview

Currently, Songversation has a friends feature allowing users to add other Songversation users and chat to them. This could be expaneded upon to allow users to verse their friends in "Guess the Song" mode.

Including multiplayer in Songversation will enhance social interaction and will foster friendly competition. Players can connect with friends, family, or strangers, sharing music tastes and battling to showcase their knowledge of artists. The game becomes a platform for discussions and conversations about music, while the competitive aspect adds excitement and motivation. Challenging others and comparing scores creates a thrilling environment where players can test their skills and experience the joy of victory.

Overall, multiplayer elevates the game by creating a dynamic and engaging experience that encourages social connection and friendly rivalry.

User Story

As a player, I want to use the multiplayer features in Songversation to connect with friends and engage in friendly competition while sharing our music tastes.

Acceptance Criteria

Test Case 1: Connecting with Friends

Given: The player is in the multiplayer lobby.

When: The player selects the option to invite friends.

Then: The player should be able to send invitations to their friends and receive confirmation when they join the game.

Test Case 2: Recieving an Invite

Given: The player is on the website.

When: Their friend sends them an invite.

Then: A notification should appear asking the player to join their friends game.

Test Case 3: Joining a Game

Given: The player has a pending invite.

When: The player clicks Join Game.

Then: The player is redirected to the game page and the game begins.

Test Case 4: Selecting an Artist or Playlist

Given: The player is in a multiplayer game.

When: It's the player's turn to choose an artist or playlist for the round.

Then: The player should be able to select an artist or playlist, and it should be reflected for all players in the game.

Test Case 5: Guessing the Song Title

Given: The players are in a multiplayer game.

When: The game presents a snippet of a song's lyrics.

Then: Each player should be able to enter their guess for the song title, and the game should validate and provide feedback on their answers.

Test Case 6: Post-Guess Screen

Given: The players are in a multiplayer game and have submitted their answers.

When: Everyone has guessed.

Then: A popup appears showing all players guessing and whether they guessed correctly along with a "Next Song" button

Test Case 7: Moving on from Guess Screen

Given: The players are in a multiplayer game and are on the post-guess screen.

When: Everyone has clicked the "Next Song" button.

Then: The next lyric snippet is displayed and each user can make their guess.

Test Case 8: Post-Game Reflection

Given: The multiplayer game has ended.

When: The players finish playing multiple rounds.

Then: A final screen is displayed, showing the score of all players along with a post game chat where they can send messages to each other.

Lyric Guessing Mode

Overview

In the lyric guessing mode, players can select a playlist or artist just like in the existing song guessing mode. However, instead of guessing the song, the website presents players with a line of lyrics from a song. Their task is to enter the next line accurately. The website then assesses their response, providing feedback on correctness. The game progresses with new lines of lyrics until the player finishes the song or they guess wrong.

This mode adds depth and challenge by testing players' knowledge of lyrics, going beyond simply guessing the songs.

User Story

As a player, I want to play the Lyric Guesser mode in Songversation to test my knowledge of artists, playlists and song lyrics.

Acceptance Criteria

Test Case 1: Choosing the mode

Given: The player is on the Songversation website.

When: The player clicks the "Lyric Guesser" game mode button.

Then: The player is redirected to a webpage where they can choose an artist or playlist from their Spotify profile.

Test Case 2: Choosing the artist or playlist

Given: The player is on the Lyric Guesser gamemode choosing an artist or playlist.

When: The player selects an artist or playlist.

Then: The player is redirected to a webpage that gives them the first lyric of a random song from that artist or playlist.

Test Case 3: Correct Guess

Given: A snippet of lyrics from a random song in the artist or playlist library is presented.

When: The player correctly guesses next lyric.

Then: The webpage awards a point to the player and displays the lyric that comes after their guess.

Test Case 4: Incorrect Guess

Given:A snippet of lyrics from a random song in the artist or playlist library is presented.

When: The player incorrectly guesses next lyric.

Then: The webpage marks the lyric as red and displays the lyric that comes after their guess.

Test Case 5: Almost Correct Guess

Given: A snippet of lyrics from a random song in the artist or playlist library is presented.

When: The player guesses next lyric but with some spelling mistakes.

Then: The game provides some leniency to the player and awards them the point, marking their guess as yellow.

Test Case 6: Skip the song

Given: A snippet of lyrics from a random song in the artist or playlist library is presented.

When: The player clicks the "Skip Song" button

Then: The game moves onto the next random song, displaying the first lyric of that song.

Test Case 7: Game Over and Final Score

Given: The player is in a lyric guesser game.

When: The game reaches the end, or the player gives up.

Then: The game should display the final score of the player, and provide an option to restart or exit the game.

Test Case 8: Responsive Design

Given: The website is available on multiple platforms.

When: The player accesses the website from a different platform.

Then: The game provides a consistent and seamless experience across platforms, with all elements appearing and functioning with no friction to the user experience.

Prioritisation

When it comes to prioritising deadlines and deliverables, especially in situations where team members have competing deadlines from other units, it is essential to establish best practices to ensure efficient workflow and successful project completion. Here are some guidelines to consider:

  1. Clearly Define Goals and Identify Minimum Viable Product:

    2. Begin by establishing a clear understanding of the project's goals and objectives. This will help prioritise tasks based on their importance.

    Evaluate what your minimum viable product (MVP) is. Determine the key deliverables that are essential for the project, and work to accomplish the MVP before working on more features.

    During the development of Songversation, the main requirement was to have a functional website that took in a users text input, and returned a response. From this requirement, we identified our MVP as "A webpage where the user can log in with Spotify, choose a playlist and guess which song the lyrics are from". This allowed us to quickly work towards a working product that met the requirements of the project before moving on to the more nice to have features.

  2. Evaluate Dependencies and Constraints:

    3. Assess the dependencies and constraints associated with each task or deliverable. Identify any tasks that rely on the completion of other tasks or require specific resources or expertise. This evaluation will help determine the order in which tasks should be prioritised.

  3. Break Down Tasks and Set Deadlines:

    4. Divide complex deliverables into smaller, manageable tasks. Assign deadlines to each task, considering factors like complexity, effort required, and dependencies. This breakdown enables better tracking of progress and allows for a more efficient allocation of resources.

    If team members have competing deadlines from other units, it may be necessary to negotiate and adjust deadlines to ensure a fair distribution of workload. Ensure that any adjustments are communicated clearly and fairly. This came up frequently in the Songversation Project with one of our members doing 4 assignments at once. Any dependancies they were working on had to be carefully evaluated and sometimes passed to more available group members so other group members can move on with the tasks.

  4. Collaborate and Communicate:

    5. Encourage open communication within the team to understand each team member's workload and competing deadlines. Ensure frequent meetings where team members can share their progress, challenges, and potential conflicts to ensure everyone is on the same page.

  5. Use Tools:

    6. Utilize project management tools such as the ones listed above. These tools are specially designed in order to maximise clarity, collaboration and communication.
